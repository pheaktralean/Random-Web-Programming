The Responsible Cat Owner's Checklist For Taking Care Of A Cat

  1. Feeding

    • Provide high-quality, brand-name cat food.
    • Ensure the diet includes taurine for heart and eye health.
    • Fresh, clean water should be available at all times.
    • Treats should not exceed 5-10% of the diet.
    • Consult your vet if signs of anorexia, diarrhea, vomiting, or lethargy persist.

  2. Grooming

    • Brush or comb your cat regularly to keep the coat clean and reduce shedding and hairballs.

  3. Handling

    • Pick up your cat by placing one hand behind the front legs and another under the hindquarters. Do not lift by the scruff or front legs.

  4. Housing

    • Provide a clean, dry, and comfortable place for your cat to sleep indoors. Outdoor cats face more risks and have shorter lifespans.

  5. Identification

    • Use a safety collar with an ID tag, and consider microchipping your cat.

  6. Litter Box

    • Place the litter box in a quiet, accessible location. Clean it daily and wash it weekly. If your cat refuses to use the litter box, consult a vet.

  7. Scratching

    • Provide a sturdy scratching post to help keep your catâ€™s claws in good condition and protect your furniture.

  8. Health

    • Schedule annual vet visits and vaccinations. Seek immediate veterinary care if your cat is sick or injured.

  9. Medicines and Poisons

    • Only give medications prescribed by a vet. Contact a vet or the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center if you suspect poisoning.

  10. Spaying and Neutering

    • Spay or neuter your cat by five months of age to prevent unwanted litters and health issues.
